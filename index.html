THUMBNAIL RULES
The non-negotiable rules extracted from the Bible V2 library. Apply all of them to every thumbnail before publishing.
01
The 3-second rule — readable at 120px wide
YouTube thumbnails appear at 120×68px on mobile — the most common view. If your text overlay isn't readable at that size, it doesn't exist. Test every thumbnail at thumbnail scale before uploading.
Zoom out your browser to 25% and look at your thumbnail. Can you still read the text? Is the main visual element still immediately clear?
Bold, high-contrast text with dark stroke or background bar
Thin font, text over busy background, more than 5 words
02
Text overlay is 3–5 words maximum. CAPS always.
The text overlay's job is not to explain the video — it's to complete the emotional circuit the title started. It must create tension, not summarise. All caps, maximum contrast, minimum words.
Title: "The Amish Secret to Keeping Pests Out Forever" → Overlay: "THEY KNEW ALL ALONG" or "ZERO PESTICIDES · EVER"
THEY HID THIS  |  FREE FOREVER  |  100 LBS · 4 SQ FT
"Watch to find out the Amish pest control method"
03
Emotion before information
The viewer's eye lands on the face or the most visually dramatic element first, then reads text second. Lead with the emotion (shock, delight, disbelief) — the information in the overlay confirms it. Never reverse this order.
Shock Face archetype: the disbelieving expression fires before the text registers. The text then answers "what are they shocked by?" — this is the click.
Face/result first, text confirms the story
Dense text block with no visual anchor
04
Colour contrast is the single biggest CTR lever
Most competing thumbnails use similar colour temperatures. Your thumbnail wins in a sea of thumbnails by being the most visually distinct. Warm/orange backgrounds dominate gardening — test cool (deep teal, navy, forest green) against warm.
Before/After Split: left side desaturated (the problem), right side vibrant green/gold (the result). The contrast sells the transformation before a word is read.
High contrast, unexpected colour for the niche, dark background with bright subject
Muddy mid-tones, same palette as every other gardening video
05
The thumbnail must deliver what the title promised
If your title says "100 lbs from 4 sq ft," the thumbnail should show the result (the massive yield, the tiny space). Mismatched thumbnails destroy trust even when the video is excellent — viewers feel misled, which tanks retention and increases dislikes.
F9 Number Shock title → Comparison Scale thumbnail showing the quantity vs tiny space. The numbers from the title should appear on the thumbnail as confirmation.
06
Run every video through A/B before committing
Never publish a video without planning a Thumbnail B challenger. Set up the YouTube Experiment the moment you publish. Your first instinct about which thumbnail will win is wrong approximately 50% of the time. The data is the only truth.
Workflow: generate 2 variants using the Brief Builder → upload Thumb A on publish → set up Experiment with Thumb B immediately → check results at 1,000 impressions per variant → keep winner permanently.
